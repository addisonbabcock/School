Project #1: Basic Gate Circuit Applications

For this project you will construct, debug, and test the following three logic circuits. You will use the knowledge gained from Lab #1 to determine IC pin numbers and breadboard the circuits.
 

1. Water Level Alarm

This simple circuit will generate an audible alarm if the probes come out of the water. The probes are pieces of wire that can be placed into a container with a small amount of water in it. The output signals Level OK and Level Low can be connected to the LED display ports of your breadboard if visual indicators are desired. It is recommended that the circuit be powered by batteries for safety considerations.
 


Figure 1  Water Level Alarm


 


2. Light Off Indicator

This circuit uses a photo cell to detect light which if present "pulls" the input of the AND gate low. The absence of light causes the voltage on respective input to go high due to the 100K pull-up resistor. If the Enable input is high the Alarm or buzzer is activated. Use the logic switch outputs of your breadboard to provide the Enable function. If visual indicators are desired, the Enable and Alarm signal can be connected to the LED display ports of your breadboard.
 
 


Figure 2  Light Off Alarm


 


3. Programmable Light/Dark Alarm

This 2-level logic circuit uses an X-OR gate implemented as a controlled inverter. The alarm circuitry can be programmed to be activated by the abscence of light or the presence of light. Use the logic switch outputs of your breadboard to provide the Enable function. If visual indicators are desired the Enable and Alarm signal can be connected to the LED display ports of your breadboard.


Figure 3  Programmable Light/Dark Alarm

 


* The 74HCT series of ICs may be used in place of the 74HC devices.
* All resistors are 1/4 watt with the tolerance not critical for these circuits.
