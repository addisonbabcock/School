Project #2: Water Level Control System

1. Aim of the Design

You have been contracted to minimally design a water level control system for the TXC industries. The logic system will monitor a number of sensors for the water level, cover off, and a reset switch. A pump and LED indicator will be controlled in the following manner:

  • The pump will turn on when the water level goes low and turn off when the vessel is filled. Two senors F and L and provided for this purpose.
  • The LED will turn when the cover is opened and stay on until the a reset is applied to the circuit.


2. External Performance (Block Diagram)

All inputs and outputs are standard logic levels (0,+5V). Logic switches and LEDs from your bread board will used to display the inputs and outputs respectively. In addition all signals are active high.

Hint: Use two SR-Nand Latches one for monitoring the F(full) and L(low) senors to control the pump. The other SR-Nand Latch will be used to monitor the C(cover) sensor and R(reset) switch to control the LED connected to output O.

4. Simulation using LogicWorks®

Enter the circuit for your design in LogicWorks® and simulate the circuit using binary switches for inputs and binary probes to monitor the output. Verify the circuit works exactly as planned by direct comparison with the simulation. If any discrepancies are found you must go back and check each step in the internal performance section of the design until the problem is located. Simulate the corrected circuit to verify the problem has been resolved. Print the schematic for your circuit (in landscape mode) and annotate with the pin numbers you wish to use. This bit of pre-planning will reduce the chance of errors when bread boarding the circuit.
 

5. Circuit Implementation

Bread board your circuit using binary switches as inputs and LEDs for the outputs. You should also connect the inputs to the LED display port. Verify the circuit performs as per the design specifications.

Once you are sure everything is correct call your instructor for a check off.
